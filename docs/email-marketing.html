The leak nobody notices

Most exterior cleaning companies spend all their money chasing new customers and zero keeping the ones they already won. The customer you washed last spring doesn't remember your name. Not because you did a bad job. Because a year is a long time and nobody wrote to them.

Winning a new customer costs ad spend. Re-booking an old one costs an email. If you've been in business more than a year, your customer list is the highest-margin marketing channel you own, and it's probably sitting in a shoebox of invoices.

What I actually send

Four campaign types, written in your voice, on a calendar that matches your season.

Seasonal reminders

"It's been a year since we washed your siding" hits different when the pollen is visibly yellow. Timed to your region's algae, pollen, and gutter seasons so the reminder lands right when the problem shows.

Review requests

Sent right after the job while the driveway still looks brand new. More reviews feed your Google Business Profile, which feeds your map ranking, which feeds you jobs without ad spend.

Referral pushes

Your happiest customers know ten neighbors with dirty driveways. A simple give-get referral offer turns one clean house into a street of them. Route density is profit.

Win-backs

Customers who booked once and went quiet get a short, honest nudge with a reason to come back. These are the cheapest jobs you'll book all year.

Fair questions, straight answers

I don't really have a customer list. Can this still work?

If you've done jobs, you have a list, it's just not organized yet. Invoices, texts, Venmo history, job addresses. Part of my setup is digging it out and turning it into something usable. If you truly have nothing, start with ads first and this becomes step two.

Isn't email dead? My customers are on Facebook.

Dead for spam, alive for reminders. A homeowner who already paid you opens an email from you. This isn't cold email to strangers, it's a note from the company that cleaned their house last year, and that's exactly why it books jobs.

How is this different from the cold outreach agencies pitch?

Cold outreach is chasing strangers. This is the opposite: warming up people who already trust you. Different list, different tone, different (much higher) booking rate.
