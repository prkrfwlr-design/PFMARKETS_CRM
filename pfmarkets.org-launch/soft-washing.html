How I think about your economics

Soft washing is a higher-ticket, education-first sale. The marketing has to sell the "why," not just the after photo.

You're selling awareness first

Most people have never heard the term "soft washing." Some think pressure washing and soft washing are the same thing, right up until a high-pressure crew cracks their siding. The ad's job is to close that gap before it asks for the sale.

Higher ticket, fewer jobs needed

A full house or roof wash runs bigger than a driveway job. That changes the math: fewer leads can hit the same revenue target, so lead quality matters more than lead volume.

Algae and mildew season is predictable

Humid Southeast summers bring the green and black streaks that make this an easy sell. I shift budget up ahead of that window instead of waiting for the phone to start ringing on its own.

Quick questions

Do people actually search for "soft washing"?

Some do, most don't yet. That's why I write ads and GBP content around the symptoms homeowners already recognize: green streaks, black stains, a roof that looks dirty no matter how much it rains. The term follows once they've clicked.

How do I show the value of a higher-ticket service in an ad?

Before/after still carries the weight, but I pair it with a short line about why high pressure would have damaged the surface. That contrast is what justifies the price to someone who's never bought this before.

Is there a seasonal push I should be budgeting for?

Yes. Algae and mildew growth ramps up through humid stretches. I move budget ahead of that window instead of reacting after the calls start coming in on their own.
